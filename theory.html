The reading "The Work of Art in the Age of the Internet" by David Carrier makes reference to Walter Benjamin’s The Work of Art in the Age of Mechanical Reproduction and challenges the concept of art, especially authenticity, because, according to Benjamin, the mechanical reproduction of a work of art is essentially defined by multiple copies, so it shatters the aura by interrupting these founding traditions. However, Carrier pushes back on this by saying, "The original physical object is now merely the source, just as the author’s manuscript is the source of a novel, and the reproduction has become a genuine replacement for that object. And so owning the original is like owning the manuscript of a novel—a thing of value for specialists but not for the general public." (Carrier, 2020:4-5). Both are valid points, and I agree with both perspectives.
